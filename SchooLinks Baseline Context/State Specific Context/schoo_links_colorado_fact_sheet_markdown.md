# SchooLinks Colorado Fact Sheet  
**How SchooLinks supports Colorado state initiatives across ICAP, Work-Based Learning (WBL), MyColoradoJourney, Course Planning, and College Application Management**

## 1) Colorado positioning (one-liner)
SchooLinks is a **student-owned college, career, and life readiness platform** that helps Colorado districts **deliver and document ICAP-quality experiences**, **scale WBL**, and **connect learning to postsecondary and workforce pathways**—while improving visibility and simplifying reporting.

---

## 2) Colorado initiatives SchooLinks supports

### A) Colorado ICAP (Individual Career & Academic Plan)
SchooLinks supports Colorado’s ICAP quality expectations by centralizing and documenting the student journey across:
- **Self-awareness** (interests, strengths, goals)
- **Career awareness & exploration** (pathways, occupations, labor market context)
- **Postsecondary aspirations & options** (college, career, military, apprenticeships)
- **Environmental expectations** (experiences beyond the classroom)
- **Academic planning** (course planning aligned to graduation + goals)
- **Employability skills** (resume/portfolio artifacts)
- **Financial literacy** (planning + awareness activities)

### B) MyColoradoJourney (MCJ) / statewide career pathway navigation
SchooLinks aligns to the MCJ vision by enabling a connected journey from:
**interests → careers → pathways → programs/training → opportunities**

Common Colorado-aligned capabilities include:
- Industry sector and pathway exploration
- Career exploration aligned to standard occupation structures (e.g., SOC)
- Regional relevance and pathway navigation

### C) Quality Career Pathway Framework (QCPF)
SchooLinks supports QCPF-aligned implementation by enabling:
1. Pathway navigation and student planning
2. Alignment to in-demand occupations and essential skills
3. Visibility into experiences, credentials, and outcomes
4. Partnerships and participation tracking for WBL and employer engagement

## 3) ICAP quality indicators → SchooLinks feature mapping

| Colorado ICAP Quality Indicator | What districts need to deliver | SchooLinks capabilities that support it |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Awareness** | Interest/strength discovery; documented goals; awareness activities | Interest inventories + assessments; goal creation/tracking; financial awareness activities; plan artifacts |
| **Career Awareness** | Clear understanding of occupations/careers; pathway knowledge | Career exploration (clusters/profiles); assessments; artifacts like resume/portfolio |
| **Postsecondary Aspirations** | Activities connected to future goals | Activity tracking; experiences; events/engagement logs |
| **Postsecondary Options** | Benchmarking; a record of applications and next steps | Goal progress; postsecondary planning; application and document tracking |
| **Environmental Expectations** | Evidence of learning beyond classroom | WBL + experience tracking (hours, verification, reflections, feedback) |
| **Academic Planning** | Multi-year planning aligned to graduation + goals | Course Planner for multi-year plans; graduation requirement alignment; pathway-aligned course planning |
| **Employability Skills** | Resume and readiness artifacts | Resume builder; portfolio artifacts; experience tracking; reflections |
| **Personal Financial Literacy** | Financial awareness and readiness | Financial literacy/awareness activities; scholarship and aid learning embedded in CCR workflows |

## 4) Colorado-ready solution areas (what districts can “buy into”)

### A) Work-Based Learning (WBL) at scale (CTE + pathways)
SchooLinks helps districts expand equitable access to WBL by combining:
- **Experience tracking** (internships, service learning, work-based experiences)
- **Verification workflows** (hours, supervisors, reflections)
- **Program management** for WBL coordination
- **Employer engagement tools** (opportunities, events, student engagement)

**Colorado tie-in:** Supports ICAP “Environmental Expectations” and helps districts operationalize pathway experiences at scale.

### B) Course Planner (graduation + academic planning)
Course Planner is a **multi-year planning tool** that helps students align courses to graduation requirements and postsecondary goals.

Key outcomes:
- Students build coherent course plans tied to goals
- Counselors gain visibility into plan progress and show readiness alignment
- Districts can support pathway-aligned planning consistently

**Colorado tie-in:** Directly supports ICAP “Academic Planning.”

### C) College Application Manager (CAM) + document workflows
CAM helps counseling teams manage the application process with less manual work by:
- Tracking application status and outcomes
- Managing document workflows (transcripts, recommendations)
- Providing visibility for students, counselors, and leadership

**Colorado tie-in:** Supports ICAP “Postsecondary Options” by keeping a clear record of applications and next steps.

### D) Personalized planning + readiness indicators (PLP/KRI-style readiness tracking)
SchooLinks supports a planning-and-indicators model where:
- Students complete required actions and artifacts in a personalized plan
- Leaders gain real-time visibility into readiness indicators and progress

**Colorado tie-in:** Supports districtwide readiness monitoring and helps reduce spreadsheet-driven reporting.
